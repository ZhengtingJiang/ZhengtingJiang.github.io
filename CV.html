    Thesis: Opinion Dynamics and Mainstream Opinion Control in Higher-Order Networks.

    Thesis Abstract: Opinions are common in daily life, work and study. Individuals would have infinite kinds of opinions on specific issues. Specifically, each individual may have a different opinion, and there are infinitely individuals in real life. Agents in the network are used to describe such abstract and huge opinion states. The edges in the network describe the pairwise relationship between agents, however, some functional units in the fields of society and biology are realized through the joint reaction of multiple individuals, and the edges of the normal network are difficult to describe such high-order relationship. This article uses hypergraphs or simplicial complex to describe the high-order relationship between individuals or agents. The article mainly combines high-order networks and binary opinion to extend the concepts and models to high-order models, exploring the impact of high-order relationships and heterogeneous groups on the opinions of individuals. The article explores the influence of individuals and groups on opinion evolution and polarization in simplicial complex, and explores the conditions for mainstream opinion models to reach the equilibrium state.
